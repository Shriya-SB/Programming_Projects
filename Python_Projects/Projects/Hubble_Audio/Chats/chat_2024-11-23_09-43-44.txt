1. **Landfilling**: Landfilling is the most common method of waste disposal worldwide. Modern landfills are equipped with a system to collect leachate and extract landfill gas, which can be used to generate electricity. However, landfills require extensive space, and there are concerns about long-term environmental implications due to the release of methane, a potent greenhouse gas contributing to climate change. Therefore, landfilling should be considered a last resort for waste disposal, and efforts should be made to reduce, reuse, and recycle waste before sending it to a landfill.
